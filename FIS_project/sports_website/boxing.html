Boxing is a combat sport where two fighters, generally of similar weight, compete using their fists with padded gloves. The objective is to score points through precise punches or achieve a knockout.

·Scoring:
     Judges score each round based on clean punches landed, effective aggression, defense, and ring control.
·Fouls:
     Actions such as hitting below the belt, holding, headbutting, or using elbows are prohibited.
·Knockout:
     If a fighter is knocked down and cannot get up before the referee counts to ten, the bout ends with a knockout.
·Decision:
     If no knockout occurs, the winner is determined by the judges' scorecards.

History Of Boxing
     Boxing has ancient origins and has evolved over millennia, with significant developments in England leading to the modern sport we know today. It has become a global phenomenon, with legendary fighters and a strong emphasis on safety and fair competition.
