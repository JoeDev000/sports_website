Tennis is a sport played as singles or doubles, where players use rackets to hit a ball over a net, aiming to score points when the opponent fails to return the ball

·Scoring:
     Points progress as 0 (love), 15, 30, 40, and game.
·Court:
     made of grass, clay, hard surfaces, or artificial turf.
·Objective:
     Win a game by scoring four points with a two-point lead; win a set by winning six games with a two-game margin.
·Grand Slams:
     The four major tournaments are the Australian Open, French Open and Wimbledon.

History Of Tennis
     Tennis originated over a thousand years ago in Europe, evolving from a medieval game played by French monks using their hands to modern lawn tennis developed in England in the late 1800s. While the game has changed significantly, it retains elements of its medieval origins. Today, tennis is a global sport with cultural significance in Europe and worldwide.
